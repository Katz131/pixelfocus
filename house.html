HOUSE

THE HOUSE · MORNING

A small house at the edge of a factory town.

The kettle is on. Somewhere in the next room a child is turning the pages of a book about a different, smaller house. The factory is a short walk away. You will be expected at the loom in an appreciable number of minutes, a fact the house absorbs without comment.

Things, at the moment, are as they should be. Not better, not worse. The light in the hallway has not yet been dimmed.
