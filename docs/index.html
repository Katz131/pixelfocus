PixelFocus

A gamified Pomodoro todo list for Chrome. Earn textiles by staying focused, weave them into pixel art, stack a factory empire, and climb seventy-four sealed title ranks from hand-loom weaver to heat-death transcendence.

What it is

PixelFocus wraps a 10-minute focus timer in a 74-rank progression system. Every completed session gives you a textile, XP toward your next rank, and once you hit Tier 3, coins that feed an upgrade-tree factory. The title ladder climbs from pre-industrial hand-loom workshops through Victorian mills, corporate oligarchy, smart factories, cyberpunk uploads, and eventually Dyson-scale cosmic megastructures — all with sardonic tooltips in the voice of a wry omniscient narrator haunting a bureaucratic office nightmare.

Titles and tier names stay sealed behind fog-of-war until you reach them. You do not get to see where the ladder is going.

Features

Wall-clock timer

Defeats Chrome's background-tab throttling by pinning the session end to Date.now().

Pop-out timer

Always-on-top floating timer via the Document Picture-in-Picture API. Works above every other window.

74 sealed ranks

Ten tiers from The Craft to Universal Transcendence. Locked titles show only “???” until earned.

Pixel art loom

Spend textiles to weave increasingly large pixel artworks. Your gallery survives extension reloads.

Factory upgrades

Tier 3+ unlocks a separate money currency and a sprawling upgrade tree with ten income engines.

Zero telemetry

Everything runs locally in chrome.storage.local. No accounts, no cloud, no network calls.

Install

  1. Click the green Download latest button above. In most cases this downloads pixelfocus-v3.19.13.zip directly to your Downloads folder. If instead it takes you to a GitHub Releases page, scroll down to the Assets section (below the release notes) and click the pixelfocus-v3.19.13.zip file there to download it.
  2. Find the downloaded zip and unzip it — right-click the file and choose Extract All on Windows, or double-click it on Mac. Put the unzipped folder somewhere you won’t accidentally delete it, like C:\PixelFocus\ or ~/PixelFocus/.
  3. Open Chrome (or Brave / Edge) and paste chrome://extensions into the address bar, then press Enter.
  4. Flip the Developer mode switch on in the top-right corner of that page.
  5. Click the Load unpacked button (top-left) and select the unzipped folder from step 2.
  6. PixelFocus is now installed. Click the puzzle-piece icon in your browser toolbar, then click the pin icon next to PixelFocus so it stays visible. Then click the PixelFocus icon to open it.
Updating later: when a new version ships, repeat steps 1–2 (download the new zip, unzip it over the old folder), then go to chrome://extensions and click the little circular reload arrow on the PixelFocus card. Your saved textiles, gallery progress, factory upgrades, and streak data all live in Chrome’s local storage and survive reloads untouched.

Privacy

PixelFocus stores everything — todo items, XP, textiles, coins, gallery, factory progress, streak history — in chrome.storage.local, which lives exclusively on your device. It does not transmit data, use analytics, phone home, or require an account. The tabs permission is used only to open the gallery and factory as full-tab pages. The notifications permission is used only to announce the end of a focus session. The alarms permission is used only to schedule end-of-session checks.
